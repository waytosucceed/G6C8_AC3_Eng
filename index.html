Adverbs


Convention/Grammar

An adverb is a word that describes a verb, an adjective or another adverb.

Adverbs of Manner describe how an action is performed or the way something happens. They answer the question "How?"

Adverbs of Time indicate when an action occurs or the time at which something happens. They answer the questions "When?" or "How often?"

Adverbs of Place indicate the location or position where an action takes place. They answer the question "Where?"

Adverbs of Frequency indicate how often an action occurs or the regularity with which something happens. They answer the question "How often?"

Adverbs of Degree indicate the extent, intensity, or degree of an action or quality. They answer the questions "To what extent?" or "How much?"

Conjunctive Adverbs are used to join two independent clauses or sentences, indicating a relationship between them. They act as connectors or transitions.


Adverb type Usage Example
Adverbs of Manner Describe how an action is performed
  • She sings beautifully.
  • He spoke softly.
Adverbs of Time Indicate when an action occurs
  • She finished her homework yesterday.
  • The train arrives soon.
Adverbs of Place Indicate where an action occurs
  • She looked everywhere.
  • They live nearby.
Adverbs of Frequency Indicate how often an action occurs
  • He always arrives on time.
  • She rarely eats out.
Adverbs of Degree Indicate the intensity or degree of an action, adjective, or another adverb
  • She is very happy.
  • He almost finished his work.
Conjunctive Adverbs Connect two independent clauses, showing relationships like contrast, cause and effect, addition, or sequence
  • It was raining heavily; therefore, the match was postponed.
  • She was tired; nevertheless, she continued working.

Word Meaning

Serenity: The state of being calm, peaceful, and untroubled.
Sentence: The quiet beach provided a sense of serenity after a stressful week at work.

Prestigious: Having a high reputation or status; highly respected and admired.
Sentence: She was accepted into a prestigious university known for its excellent academic programs.

Meticulously: In a way that shows great attention to detail; very carefully and precisely.
Sentence: The surgeon meticulously prepared for the operation, ensuring everything was in order.
